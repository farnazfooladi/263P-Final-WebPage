Problems

Because of Covid-19 impact, mobile ordering is the safest one for everyone when they want to order food since it involves little to no human contact. That has accelerated adoption by both restaurants and consumers. Given the changing scenarios, consumers have come to expect the ability of a restaurant to allow them to place orders online and get delivery. But sometimes the delivery and service fee will cost more than the price of single food, so we want to design a system that provides free lunch and dinner delivery services to specific locations in Irvine, San Diego and Los Angeles.

Methods

Competitor Analysis

During the user evaluation phase, we chose 4 existing food delivery systems which solved different parts of the problem we mentioned in the proposal. By analyzing these 4 competitors, we made cases for the market niche we are serving and compared various approaches that have already been tried.

Interview for user evaluation

During the user evaluation phase, we interviewed our potential users to better understand the needs of the actual users. First, we drew up an interview protocol, most of questions were concise, open-ended questions to encourage participants to tell their stories. After the interviews, we categorized the user's answers and compiled a list of their needs.

Design prototype & Interview

After designing both the static version and the interactive version of the wireframe, we interviewed 6 people to better understand the usability of this prototype. We designed a series of tasks to complete in our prototype for interviewees. After the interview, We collected all of their feedback and set priorities for all of these usability problems.

Heuristic Evaluation & high-fidelity mockup

After the prototype interview, we used Nielsen's 10 Heuristics to inspect every page in the prototype. We gathered everyone's results, identified a list of issues to be solved, and designed our high-fidelity mockup based on this list.

Results

Competitive analysis

Postmates

Postmates is one of many on-demand delivery companies in the United States providing delivery from restaurants and stores. It provides services of receiving food or products right at their door to users; users will be paying both food or products fees and delivery fees to the driver.

UberEats

Uber Eats is an online food ordering and delivery platform powered by the same technology that Uber uses. Customers can have food delivered on-demand from a selection of hundreds of restaurants in major cities.

Rhea Mommy's Kitchen

Rheamommy's kitchen is a food delivery application that provides a day-to-day menu for users to order meals, so users can pick their favorite combinations of food within a range of choices.

HelloFresh

HelloFresh is a delivery service that provides the ingredients needed for a meal to customers who can cook the meal using pre-prepared recipe cards.

Understanding of your users and your personas

Through these interviews, we have identified many aspects that are very important to the user. Many of these aspects are the same as the ones we had in mind before our interviews. Most people prefer to eat at home, which indicates a large potential market for food delivery services.People do not like to pay extra fees, they rather pay a good tip and only a small amount for the delivery fee. They like the restaurant to pay all the service fees instead of customers. They also like to have some new features such as tracking delivery. Having a variety of people from different demographics helped us to understand and make sure what exactly users need in reality. All these new features and dislikes will help us to have a better understanding of the user's needs in order to have a great outcome.

Evaluation results

Some of the major problems we fixed during our user and heuristic evaluation are:
1. Another feedback for the back button is to use an arrow (←) which is more common and universal instead of a square with “Back” inside.
2. The ‘Back’ button is not in every page, for example, the Cart page. So when the user opens the Cart page by mistake, he cannot go back to the previous page.
3. In other digital products, pop-up windows can be closed by clicking the ‘X’ button, but in our system, only clicking outside of pop-up windows can close them.
4. Having a menu button is ambiguous to users, they can not recall that the menu in the food delivery app is for more options or actual menu for the food.
5. There is no total amount in the check out page. The total amount only in the Cart page.Users do not know how much they should pay for this order.
